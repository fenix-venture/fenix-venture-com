01 — Positioning

The one-sentence version

Everything Fenix does flows from one core belief: the best way to acquire a business is to truly understand it first - not from a spreadsheet, but from inside.

"We buy businesses the way people buy homes. We walk through them, imagine ourselves living in them, and plan what we'd change — before we ever make an offer."

Core positioning statement. Use this as the filter for all creative decisions.

The consulting work is how we get there. We embed in a business as an advisor, which gives us access to its people, processes, and real problems — the things no data room ever shows. Even if we don't acquire, every client leaves in better shape than when we arrived. That's the value exchange, and it's what sets Fenix apart from every other buyer in the market.

02 — Audience

Who we're talking to

Our primary audience is operators and business owners who are considering, or open to, being acquired. They are skeptical of financial buyers who see them as a line item. They want a partner who actually understands what they've built.

The Founder-Operator

Built their business over 10-20 years. Owns it. Wants to know the buyer actually cares about what they've built, not just the EBITDA.

The Legacy Thinker

Thinking about the next chapter for themselves and their team. Wants continuity, not a strip-and-flip. A cultural fit matters as much as price.

The Growth-Stuck Operator

Good business, real potential, but stuck. Open to a partner who can bring capital, systems, and execution - not just a check.

03 — Voice & Tone

How we sound

Fenix sounds like a trusted advisor who has done this before and is not trying to impress you with jargon. We are direct, human, and confident. We speak plainly because plain is powerful.

We sound like this

  • Direct and plainspoken
  • Confident without being arrogant
  • Human and relational - like a conversation
  • Specific - no vague promises
  • Operator-to-operator

Not like this

  • PE-firm formal ("leverage our synergies")
  • Vague and hedged ("solutions for growth")
  • Salesy or pushy
  • Acronym-heavy or jargon-dense
  • Cold or clinical

06 — Key Messages

What we always say

These four messages should be present in some form on every major page. They are the proof points behind our positioning.

  • 01
    We buy businesses like people buy homes. Not off a balance sheet. We walk through them, meet the team, and plan what we'd change before we ever make an offer. This is the core differentiator.
  • 02
    The consulting work is the test drive. Our advisory engagement is how we earn the right to buy. We embed in your business, find the real issues, and prove we can help - before asking for anything.
  • 03
    You win either way. Whether we acquire or not, every business we work with leaves in better shape than when we arrived. That's not a pitch - it's what we're accountable to.
  • 04
    We bring the whole toolkit. Strategy, execution, fractional leadership, AI and automation (via GetLatest.ai) - we're not just financial buyers. We're operators who can actually fix things.
